Tax-Filing

: Income tax return is a form that needs to be filed by every individual and entity to declare their income, claim deductions and pay taxes.

ITR Filing

Any individual or entity whose gross total income exceeds the exemption limit, even if the tax liability is zero, is required to file an income tax return.The different types of income tax returns are ITR-1, ITR-2, ITR-3, ITR-4, ITR-5, ITR-6, and ITR-7, each applicable for different categories of taxpayers. (Keywords: types, income tax returns.The due date for filing income tax return is usually July 31st of the assessment year for individuals and September 30th for entities like companies.Non-filing or delayed filing of income tax return can result in penalty, interest, and legal consequences, including prosecution in some cases.You can file an income tax return after the due date, but it will be considered as a belated return, and you may have to pay a penalty and interest.The documents required for filing income tax return include Form 16, salary slips, bank statements, investment proofs, and other relevant documents that show the income earned and tax paid during the financial year.

The deductions available under income tax include Section 80C, Section 80D, Section 80G, Section 80E, and various other sections, which provide deductions for investments, medical expenses, donations, education loans, and other expenses. Form 16 is a document that employers provide to their employees, which contains details of the salary earned, tax deducted, and other relevant information. It can be used for filing income tax return by calculating the taxable income and claiming the deduction.You can e-file income tax return online by visiting the income tax e-filing website, registering yourself, and filling out the relevant form. You can also use third-party websites or software for e-filing income tax return.

5 steps to file Income tax returns

1. Gather Documents: The first step is to gather all the necessary documents such as Form 16, Form 26AS, bank statements, and investment proofs. These documents will help you in calculating your total income and tax liability.

2. Calculate Total Income and Tax Liability: Next, calculate your total income and tax liability for the financial year. You can use the income tax calculator available on the Income Tax Department's website to do this.

3. Choose ITR Form: Based on your income sources and nature of income, choose the relevant ITR form from the seven available forms. Ensure that you choose the correct form to avoid any penalty.

4. File ITR Online: After choosing the ITR form, file your ITR online by logging in to the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal. Fill in the required details such as personal information, income details, and tax payable, and submit the return.

5. Verify ITR: Once you have filed your ITR, verify it either electronically through Aadhaar OTP, Net banking, or by sending a signed copy of the ITR-V to the Income Tax Department within 120 days of filing.

GST FILING

GST is a consumption-based tax that is levied on the supply of goods and services in India. It replaced multiple indirect taxes like VAT, Service Tax, etc. Any individual or entity whose turnover exceeds Rs. 20 lakhs (Rs. 10 lakhs for special category states) in a financial year is required to register for GST. The different types of GST returns are GSTR-1, GSTR-2A, GSTR-3B, GSTR-4, GSTR-5, GSTR-6, GSTR-7, GSTR-8, GSTR-9, and GSTR-9C, each applicable for different categories of taxpayers.The due date for filing GST returns varies depending on the type of return and the turnover of the taxpayer. It is usually the 10th, 15th, or 20th of the following month.Non-filing or delayed filing of GST returns can result in a penalty, interest, and suspension of GST registration.You can file GST returns after the due date by paying a late fee and interest, but it is better to file the returns within the due date to avoid any penalty or interest. The documents required for filing GST returns include invoices, purchase bills, bank statements, and other relevant documents that show the details of the supplies made or received during the tax period. Input Tax Credit (ITC) is the credit that can be claimed by a registered taxpayer for the tax paid on inputs used in the production of goods or services. It can be set off against the tax liability of the taxpayer. You can make GST payments online by logging in to the GST portal, generating a challan, and making the payment through net banking, credit card, or debit card.You can file GST returns online by logging in to the GST portal, selecting the relevant return, filling out the required details, and submitting the return. You can also use third-party software or apps for filing GST returns.

An individual must file his ITR when the aggregate amount of TDS & TCS is Rs. 25,000. This limit of 25000 will change to 50000 for senior citizen individuals.An individual must file his ITR when cumulative Deposits in one or more Savings Bank Accounts is equal to or more than 50 lacs in that financial year.

5 Steps file GST returns

1. Register for GST: The first step is to register for GST if you haven't already done so. You can register for GST on the GST portal by providing your PAN, mobile number, and email address.

2. Collect Invoices: Collect all the invoices and bills related to the sale and purchase of goods and services during the tax period. Ensure that they contain all the necessary details such as GSTIN, HSN or SAC codes, and taxable value

3. File GSTR-1: After collecting all the invoices, file GSTR-1 by providing the details of all the outward supplies made during the tax period. GSTR-1 needs to be filed by the 11th of the following month.

4. File GSTR-3B: Next, file GSTR-3B by providing the details of all the inward and outward supplies made during the tax period, along with the Input Tax Credit (ITC) claimed. GSTR-3B needs to be filed by the 20th of the following month

5. Pay Tax and File GSTR-9: Finally, pay the tax liability for the tax period and file GSTR-9, which is an annual return that consolidates all the monthly returns filed during the financial year. GSTR-9 needs to be filed by the 31st of December of the following financial year
